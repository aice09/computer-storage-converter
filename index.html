History and Recognition

This page describes the history, recognition, and standards related to computer data storage units:

  • Bit: Smallest unit of information, introduced in the 1940s.
  • Nibble: 4 bits, used for hexadecimal representation.
  • Byte (B): 8 bits, standard unit for storage.
  • Binary Units (IEC): Introduced by IEC in 1998, multiples of 1024 (KiB, MiB, etc.).
  • Decimal Units (SI): Standardized by ISO/IEC, multiples of 1000 (KB, MB, etc.).
